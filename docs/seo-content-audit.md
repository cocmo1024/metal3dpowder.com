# SEO Content Audit and Execution Notes

Date: 2026-04-25

## Current Inventory

- Published blog articles: 71 before this round.
- Main coverage: titanium, stainless steel, nickel alloys, copper alloys, tool steel, aluminum, cobalt chrome, LPBF, EBM, DED, MIM, and binder jet.
- Strongest clusters: Ti64 aerospace and medical, CuCrZr thermal and electrical hardware, 316L/17-4PH stainless production parts, H13/M300 tooling, IN718/IN625 high-temperature and corrosion hardware.
- Structural foundation: material hubs, application hubs, process hubs, comparison page, knowledge page, tag archives, related articles, previous/next navigation, sitemap, RSS feed, and article schema are already present.

## Quality Risks Found

- Many early articles are useful but short. The average body length is around 335 words, and multiple pages are below 300 words.
- The site should not solve this by mass-producing near-duplicate pages. Google explicitly rewards helpful, reliable, people-first content and warns against scaled pages created mainly to manipulate rankings.
- The safer route is to expand by search intent clusters: material + application + process + buying decision.
- The site should keep each article narrow enough to rank for a clear query, but complete enough to help an engineer or sourcing buyer make the next decision.

## Content Principles Going Forward

- Every new article must answer a real powder search intent, not a generic industry trend.
- Every article should connect the application back to powder selection, process fit, particle-size expectations, inspection risk, and RFQ information.
- Internal links should connect from narrow application articles back to material guides and adjacent application pages.
- Avoid supplier-switching, procurement bureaucracy, and generic business content unless it directly helps powder buyers.
- Avoid exaggerated claims, future-looking hype, or unsupported material performance statements.

## Next Expansion Buckets

- Hot applications: AI liquid cooling, power electronics, EV manufacturing fixtures, eVTOL, satellite hardware, semiconductor and clean-flow equipment, hydrogen combustion hardware.
- Production applications: MIM and binder jet stainless parts, injection molding and die-casting tooling, dental and medical titanium/cobalt chrome, corrosion-side manifolds.
- Material decision pages: Ti64 vs CP titanium, Grade 23 vs Grade 5, 316L vs 17-4PH, IN718 vs IN625, CuCrZr vs pure copper, H13 vs M300, CoCrMo vs stainless.
- Process decision pages: LPBF PSD windows, EBM powder size, DED powder versus wire, MIM/binder jet powder expectations.
