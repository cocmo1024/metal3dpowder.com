# Metal Powder Industry Opportunity Map

## Scope

This map is for `metal3dpowder.com`.

The site should attract people who are actively looking for powder, not generic AM curiosity traffic.

That means content should stay anchored to:

- alloy name,
- powder form,
- process route,
- application class.

## What matters most

There are four useful buckets for powder traffic:

1. high-value certified applications,
2. high-volume production applications,
3. fast-growing frontier applications,
4. process-led powder searches.

## Highest-value sectors

These are the sectors where one approved powder route can be worth a lot, even if total search volume is not the highest.

### Aerospace and space

Strong materials:

- Ti64 / Ti64 Grade 23,
- IN718,
- IN625,
- Hastelloy X,
- CuCrZr for selected hot copper hardware.

Strong application patterns:

- hydraulic manifolds,
- valve blocks,
- hot-section brackets,
- exhaust hardware,
- combustion or hot-gas hardware,
- heat exchangers.

Why this matters:

- qualification barriers are high,
- technical searchers usually already know the alloy family,
- search intent is often close to project definition rather than casual browsing.

### Medical and dental

Strong materials:

- Ti64 Grade 23,
- TA1 / Grade 2 titanium,
- CoCrMo.

Strong application patterns:

- orthopedic implants,
- spinal devices,
- dental frameworks,
- crowns and bridges,
- surgical or dental production hardware.

Why this matters:

- powder choice is tied to grade, chemistry, and process discipline,
- the traffic is lower-volume than generic titanium terms but commercially stronger.

## Highest-volume production sectors

These are the sectors where part counts and recurring demand are high.

### MIM and binder jet stainless production

Strong materials:

- 316L,
- 17-4PH.

Strong application patterns:

- medical devices,
- automotive parts,
- industrial stainless components,
- filters,
- corrosion-side hardware,
- structural stainless parts.

Why this matters:

- many production systems still use MIM-style powder language,
- searchers often include process terms such as `metal injection molding`, `MIM`, or `binder jet`.

### Tooling and injection molding

Strong materials:

- H13,
- M300.

Strong application patterns:

- conformal cooling inserts,
- die-casting inserts,
- mold inserts,
- production tooling,
- compact core inserts.

Why this matters:

- recurring industrial demand,
- clear ROI case,
- good match between powder search intent and application-led content.

## Fast-growing frontier applications

These are not always the highest-volume searches, but they are strong authority builders and often lead to better-fit inquiries.

### Copper thermal and electrical hardware

Strong materials:

- CuCrZr,
- pure copper where the site later chooses to cover it.

Strong application patterns:

- heat exchangers,
- induction coils,
- current-carrying parts,
- cold plates,
- conductive tooling,
- rocket-engine copper parts,
- e-mobility copper hardware.

### Lightweight aluminum and titanium structures

Strong materials:

- AlSi10Mg,
- Ti64.

Strong application patterns:

- UAV structures,
- electronics housings,
- lightweight brackets,
- lattice-enabled nodes,
- flight-control hydraulic hardware.

## How powder searchers usually search

Without Search Console or Ads data, the safest working model is to infer query style from current supplier titles, process documentation, and industry naming.

The dominant search patterns are:

### 1. Alloy + powder

Examples:

- `ti64 powder`
- `316l powder`
- `17-4ph powder`
- `in718 powder`
- `cucrzr powder`

### 2. Alloy + process + powder

Examples:

- `ti64 powder lpbf`
- `316l powder mim`
- `17-4ph powder metal injection molding`
- `316l powder binder jet`
- `in625 powder ded`

### 3. Alloy + application + powder

Examples:

- `ti64 powder hydraulic manifold`
- `grade 23 titanium powder medical implant`
- `316l powder manifold`
- `cucrzr powder induction coil`
- `alsi10mg powder housing`

### 4. Specification-led powder searches

Examples:

- `15-45 um titanium powder`
- `15-53 um 316l powder`
- `grade 23 titanium powder`
- `spherical in718 powder`
- `gas atomized 17-4ph powder`

## Content rule from this map

If a topic cannot be expressed as:

- alloy,
- powder,
- process,
- application,

then it is probably not core content for this site.
