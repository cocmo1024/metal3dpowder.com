H13 tool steel powder is typically evaluated for dies, inserts, and production tooling that must tolerate thermal cycling and repeated mechanical use. In additive manufacturing, it is relevant when tooling performance and turnaround both matter.

## Why H13 is not just another steel powder

The buying logic for H13 is tied to the end-use tool. Thermal exposure, wear mechanism, and heat-treatment route all matter. A buyer should not assume that H13 can be sourced or processed exactly like 316L or general structural steels.

## Powder and process fit

Particle size range, chemistry discipline, and downstream heat treatment need to be defined together. The powder purchase is only one part of the tooling workflow. If the build will be machined, hardened, or surface-treated afterward, that should be clear in the RFQ.

## RFQ checklist

- Target process such as LPBF
- PSD window
- Heat-treatment expectations
- Tooling application and service condition
- Required documents and delivery destination

That information makes H13 quoting more accurate and reduces rework after the first technical review.
