Ti64 powder becomes especially relevant when the part is not just an aerospace bracket, but a hydraulic manifold or valve-block problem that also needs structural titanium performance. These parts usually care about weight, internal routing, pressure-side geometry, and part consolidation at the same time.

## Why this is a high-value titanium application

Aircraft hydraulic hardware is one of the clearest cases where additive manufacturing changes the part definition itself. A valve block or manifold can move from drilled passages and multiple functional elements into one tighter titanium geometry.

That is why Ti64 keeps showing up in:

- aircraft hydraulic manifolds,
- valve blocks,
- pressure-side aerospace routing hardware,
- structural titanium parts with internal flow logic.

## Why Ti64 fits this job

The application is still structural titanium work. The part must survive aerospace loading logic, but it also gains value from internal routing freedom and part-count reduction. Ti64 powder remains a natural fit because it combines broad aerospace familiarity with a mature LPBF route.

## What makes manifolds different from ordinary brackets

The engineering question is not only strength. It is also:

- internal channel layout,
- sealing and machining strategy,
- pressure-path consolidation,
- how many cross-drilled or assembled features can be eliminated.

That is why a hydraulic manifold or valve block is usually a better titanium AM use case than a part that is simply flat and easy to machine conventionally.

## When to stay inside the Ti64 family

If the part is still a flight- or pressure-side titanium component, the right next question is usually Grade 5 versus Grade 23, not whether to leave the Ti64 family entirely. The powder route should stay tied to the service condition and certification logic.

## The practical takeaway

Use Ti64 powder when the part is an aircraft hydraulic manifold or valve block that gains real value from internal routing freedom, part consolidation, and structural titanium performance.
