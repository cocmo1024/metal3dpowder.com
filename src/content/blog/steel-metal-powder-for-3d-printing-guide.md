Steel metal powder for 3D printing covers several different alloy routes. A buyer searching for "steel powder" may actually need corrosion resistance, strength, tool-life improvement, mold cooling, or production part scalability. Those are different powder decisions.

The most common steel-related AM powder conversations include 316L stainless steel, 17-4PH stainless steel, H13 tool steel, and M300 maraging steel.

## 316L stainless steel powder

316L is often the first stainless powder to review when corrosion resistance, ductility, and process stability matter more than peak strength. It is widely used for manifolds, housings, fluid-path hardware, prototypes, filters, and corrosion-aware industrial parts.

316L is not the strongest steel powder route. Its advantage is usually stainless corrosion behavior, ductility, and a mature AM process window.

## 17-4PH stainless steel powder

17-4PH is a precipitation-hardening stainless powder used when the part needs more strength than 316L typically provides. It can fit stronger fixtures, industrial hardware, workholding parts, valve-adjacent hardware, and binder jet production parts.

The key difference is heat treatment. 17-4PH final properties depend on the correct aging route and process history, so the buyer should discuss heat-treatment assumptions before production.

## H13 tool steel powder

H13 is a hot-work tool steel powder used for dies, inserts, conformal cooling tooling, injection molding tools, and die-casting inserts. It is relevant when thermal fatigue, heat checking, and hot-work stability matter.

H13 is not simply a stronger stainless steel. It is a tooling material and should be selected when the part is actually a tool or hot-work insert.

## M300 maraging steel powder

M300 is a maraging steel powder used for high-strength tooling, compact mold inserts, core inserts, and precision tool components. It is often reviewed when dimensional stability and aged strength matter in mold or tooling hardware.

Compared with H13, M300 is usually more relevant to precision tooling and compact insert logic, while H13 is more hot-work and thermal-fatigue focused.

## How to choose the right steel powder

Use 316L when corrosion resistance and a stable stainless route dominate. Use 17-4PH when stainless strength matters more. Use H13 when the application is a hot-work tool, die, or thermal-cycling insert. Use M300 when compact high-strength tooling and aging response are central.

The powder inquiry should define alloy grade, process route, particle-size expectation, quantity, heat-treatment need, and whether the part is prototype, qualification, or recurring production.

## Process route changes the powder question

For LPBF, the buyer usually discusses a fine powder cut and build stability. For binder jet or MIM, sintering behavior, part shrinkage, and production repeatability become more important. For tooling inserts, heat treatment and finish machining may matter more than the powder order itself.

This is why "steel powder for 3D printing" should quickly become a more specific inquiry: 316L LPBF manifold, 17-4PH binder jet hardware, H13 conformal cooling insert, or M300 mold tooling route.

Steel metal powder for 3D printing should be selected by application, not by the word "steel."
