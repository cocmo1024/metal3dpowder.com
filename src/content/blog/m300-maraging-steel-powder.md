M300 is one of the most useful AM steels for precision tooling. Teams often come to it when they need high strength, high hardness after aging, and a practical route for mold inserts or conformal cooling components. In additive manufacturing, that combination makes it one of the most widely discussed tooling powders.

## Why M300 is popular in AM

M300 is commonly used for inserts, molds, and tooling geometries where AM delivers clear design value through internal cooling or shape simplification. The material is attractive because the tooling case is often easier to justify than with more exotic alloys: the project wants harder, stronger tooling with geometry that conventional machining struggles to deliver.

That makes M300 a frequent fit for:

- injection mold inserts
- conformal cooling components
- precision tooling
- high-strength workshop and production aids

## Why teams choose it instead of H13

M300 and H13 are not interchangeable. The choice usually depends on the thermal severity of the job.

M300 is often preferred when the project values:

- high strength after aging
- tooling precision
- mold insert performance
- conformal cooling benefits

H13 is often the better answer when the tool will see more aggressive hot-work duty and repeated thermal cycling. That is why the right comparison is usually not "which steel is better" but "which service condition are we really designing for."

## Powder quality is not a minor detail

Because M300 is commonly used in tooling, consistency matters. Stable PSD, chemistry control, morphology, and batch traceability all influence whether the material can move from trial builds into repeatable insert production.

That is why "maraging steel powder" alone is not enough as a description. The tooling objective and service condition still have to be explicit.

## The practical takeaway

Use M300 when the project is tooling-driven and the value comes from high post-aged strength, insert performance, and conformal cooling geometry.
