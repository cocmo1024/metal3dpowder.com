M300 maraging steel powder is widely considered for mold inserts, precision tooling, and industrial support components that need high strength after aging. The material is attractive when the buyer values predictable tooling performance and a mature post-processing path.

## Why M300 is common in tooling workflows

Maraging steel is often selected because the powder can support detailed printed geometry while the final mechanical response is developed through heat treatment. That makes it useful for conformal cooling inserts and other tooling applications where geometry and strength both matter.

## What matters before ordering

The supplier should know the intended process route, PSD window, and planned heat treatment. Quoting M300 as a generic steel powder leaves too much risk in the job because the final application usually depends on post-build processing.

## Questions worth confirming

- LPBF or other target process
- Particle size range
- Aging or other heat-treatment expectations
- Insert, mold, or tooling use case
- Quantity, documents, and destination

When those inputs are defined early, M300 procurement aligns better with real production tooling requirements.
