Ti64 Grade 23 powder becomes important when the project is not just asking for titanium, but for a more controlled ELI-style titanium route suited to medical hardware. These parts usually care about ductility, toughness, biocompatibility, and tighter chemistry intent more than they care about squeezing maximum strength out of a standard Ti64 route.

## Where Grade 23 usually enters

This route is most relevant for:

- orthopedic implants,
- spinal devices,
- medical titanium components,
- controlled medical-development hardware.

These are not generic titanium applications. They are applications where the titanium grade definition matters early.

## Why Grade 23 is different from standard Ti64

Grade 23 stays inside the Ti-6Al-4V family, but with lower oxygen and iron than a standard Ti64 route. That usually means better elongation and toughness with somewhat lower strength. For medical and implant-side reasoning, that trade is often the point.

## Why this is a high-value powder topic

Medical titanium programs are rarely casual. The project is usually already thinking about:

- grade intent,
- release logic,
- biocompatibility-driven material choice,
- whether the route should stay inside Ti64 ELI rather than broad titanium language.

That makes Grade 23 one of the strongest titanium powder topics for authority-building content.

## When not to force Grade 23

If the part is really an aerospace or industrial structural component without medical-grade titanium logic, a broader [TC4 / Ti64 powder route](/posts/Alloys/tc4-ti6al4v-powder/) may be enough. Grade 23 should be used when the application actually needs the tighter titanium definition.

## The practical takeaway

Use Ti64 Grade 23 powder when the application is an orthopedic implant or medical titanium component that needs a tougher, cleaner ELI-style Ti64 route.
