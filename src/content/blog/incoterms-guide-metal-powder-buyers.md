International powder buyers make a costly mistake when they compare quotes without normalizing the delivery term. The visible price may look better, but the real cost and risk can be sitting inside a different shipping obligation.

ICC remains the official publisher of the Incoterms(R) 2020 rules. For a powder buyer, that matters because the named rule and named place directly shape who arranges transport, who carries risk at each stage, and what the quote really includes.

## Why this matters in AM powder sourcing

Powder orders are often international, document-heavy, and time-sensitive. A buyer comparing two offers needs to know whether the supplier is quoting on a basis that stops at origin, covers export handling, or extends all the way toward the destination.

Without that clarity, a lower quote can easily become the more expensive one.

## The rule buyers should follow

Never compare two international powder quotes until the delivery term is normalized.

That means checking:

- the Incoterms(R) 2020 rule
- the named place
- who arranges carriage
- who carries export-side obligations
- where risk transfers

If those differ, the headline price is not a valid comparison.

## Why named place matters

The named place is not a decorative detail. Under ICC guidance, the rule is meaningful only when the place is clearly defined. A vague delivery basis creates room for misunderstanding about handoff point, cost scope, and carrier responsibility.

That is especially risky when the shipment includes controlled documents, powder handling expectations, or timing-sensitive customs work.

## How buyers should use Incoterms(R) 2020 in RFQs

If the buyer already knows the desired delivery basis, state the rule and named place in the RFQ. If not, at least say what the supplier is expected to cover operationally:

- export handling only
- main carriage coordination
- destination delivery support
- document support for import clearance

That is usually enough to prevent the supplier from building the quote on the wrong assumption.

For international powder buying, the right Incoterms(R) 2020 rule is part of the product definition. If the delivery term is unclear, the quote is unclear. Normalize the rule and named place before treating one offer as cheaper than another.
