Titanium Grade 2 powder is a commercially pure titanium route used when corrosion resistance, chemical stability, and ductility matter more than the higher strength associated with Ti-6Al-4V. It is relevant for industrial and chemical-service components where the buyer wants titanium performance without defaulting to aerospace alloys.

## Where Grade 2 makes sense

Grade 2 is usually considered when the part will see corrosive media, marine exposure, or a process environment where simpler chemistry is preferable. It is not a substitute for Ti64 in every job. The decision depends on what the part actually needs from the finished material.

## Powder considerations

As with other reactive titanium powders, buyers should confirm PSD, oxygen control, morphology, and packaging conditions before treating the material as interchangeable. Typical LPBF-oriented windows include 15-45 um and 15-53 um, but the correct cut depends on the machine and process route.

## Questions to settle before quoting

- Which additive process will be used
- Required PSD range
- Quantity, packaging size, and destination
- Any chemistry, documentation, or compliance requirements

If those details are clear at the start, Titanium Grade 2 powder can be quoted against an actual application instead of a generic titanium request.
