TC4 is the Chinese designation commonly aligned with Ti-6Al-4V. It remains the default titanium alloy for many additive manufacturing programs because it balances strength, weight, corrosion resistance, and process maturity better than almost any other titanium grade in serial AM work.

## Why TC4 stays dominant

The reason is not only material performance. TC4 has a mature supply chain, well-understood PSD windows, broad printer parameter availability, and widely accepted post-processing routes. That reduces qualification risk compared with less common titanium systems.

Grade 5 and Grade 23 are usually the most relevant references. Grade 23 uses tighter interstitial control and is commonly preferred when higher ductility or more demanding fatigue performance is required. Grade 5 is still widely used when the part is strength-driven and the project does not need extra-low interstitial limits.

## Powder points that matter

For LPBF, buyers often request 15-45 um or 15-53 um. EBM usually moves to a coarser range, while DED can require a very different cut depending on nozzle design and feed method. The correct answer is process-specific rather than universal.

Titanium procurement should never stop at PSD alone. Oxygen, nitrogen, morphology, satellite level, and packing behavior directly affect how safely the powder can be introduced into a qualification workflow. If the project includes reuse, baseline chemistry becomes even more important.

## Process fit

LPBF is the most common request because TC4 prints dense parts with strong geometry freedom and a large installed machine base. EBM is often chosen when residual stress management and thicker sections matter more than surface finish. DED is useful for larger structures, repairs, or geometry build-up where powder feed behavior becomes part of the process risk.

## What to confirm before ordering

- Required alloy reference: Grade 5, Grade 23, or project-specific chemistry window
- Target process: LPBF, EBM, or DED
- Particle size range
- Oxygen and nitrogen limits
- Packaging size, documents, and destination country

If you send those items in the first inquiry, the quotation can be scoped around actual engineering requirements rather than generic titanium pricing.
