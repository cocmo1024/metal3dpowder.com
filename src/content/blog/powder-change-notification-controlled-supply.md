Controlled AM supply breaks down quickly when the buyer assumes the supplier will "obviously" report meaningful changes. In practice, that expectation needs to be written down. If it is not, the first surprise usually arrives at the wrong moment: during receiving, requalification, or an urgent replenishment cycle.

That is why change-notification rules should be treated as part of the supply specification, not as an informal courtesy.

## Why change notification matters more in recurring programs

In an early prototype order, the buyer may still be learning the material route. In a recurring or qualification-sensitive program, the release basis is already more controlled. That means even a reasonable supplier-side change can create internal review work if the program was not expecting it.

The purpose of change notification is not to freeze the supplier forever. It is to make sure changes do not arrive silently inside a supposedly controlled supply relationship.

## What buyers usually want notified

The exact list depends on the program, but buyers often want prior notice around changes that affect:

- release documentation
- lot traceability logic
- packaging format
- shipment structure
- any specification element the qualified basis depends on

The requirement should be specific enough that both sides know what triggers a review rather than a routine shipment.

## Why this connects directly to supplier onboarding

Change-notification rules become especially important when a buyer is adding or managing more than one approved source. The second source question is not only whether the supplier can ship the powder. It is whether the supplier can operate inside the same change-discipline expectation. That is why the [alternate supplier onboarding checklist](/posts/Alloys/alternate-supplier-onboarding-qualified-powder-programs/) is the right companion for multi-source programs.

## The document handoff has to support the rule

Change notification does not work well if the qualification basis is still scattered across email history and rough RFQs. Buyers should be able to point to a controlled document handoff that defines what the supplier is supporting. The [prototype-to-qualification handoff guide](/posts/Alloys/prototype-to-qualification-powder-document-handoff/) remains the best foundation for that.

## What a practical requirement looks like

A usable change-notification rule should answer:

1. what changes require prior notice
2. how much advance notice is expected
3. who on the buyer side receives the notice
4. whether the change affects open or future releases
5. when a new internal review is required

If those points are not explicit, the notification rule is too vague to protect the program.

## The practical takeaway

Change notification is one of the simplest ways to turn recurring powder supply into a controlled commercial relationship. Buyers should not assume the supplier knows what matters. Define it, tie it to the qualification basis, and make sure the rule is compatible with alternate sources, staged releases, and repeat orders.
