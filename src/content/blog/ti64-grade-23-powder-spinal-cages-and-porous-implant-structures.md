Ti64 Grade 23 powder becomes especially relevant when the implant hardware is not just a generic medical titanium component, but a spinal cage or porous implant structure that depends on controlled titanium chemistry and geometry. These parts are where the ELI-style route becomes easier to justify on application logic alone.

## Why spinal and porous implants point to Grade 23

These parts usually care about:

- implant-side toughness and ductility,
- tighter chemistry intent,
- lattice or porous geometry,
- a titanium route that stays inside medical-grade logic.

That is why Grade 23 keeps showing up when the discussion moves from general medical titanium to real implant programs.

## Why this is narrower than a broad medical page

A broad medical titanium article can include many devices. Spinal cages and porous implant structures are narrower because the part geometry, the material cleanliness, and the implant-side performance logic are all tied together.

## When Grade 23 is not the point

If the titanium part is not actually implant-side or does not need the tighter ELI-style route, a broader Ti64 path may be enough. Grade 23 should be used where the application truly needs what the tighter grade definition changes.

## The practical takeaway

Choose Ti64 Grade 23 powder for spinal cages and porous implant structures when the project needs implant-side titanium logic rather than a generic structural titanium route.
