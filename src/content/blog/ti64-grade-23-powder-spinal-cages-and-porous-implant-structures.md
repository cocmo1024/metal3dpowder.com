Ti64 Grade 23 powder becomes especially relevant when the implant hardware is not just a generic medical titanium component, but a spinal cage or porous implant structure that depends on controlled titanium chemistry and geometry. These parts are where the ELI-style route becomes easier to justify on application logic alone.

This page is intentionally narrow. It is not a general medical-device article and it is not a clinical recommendation. It is a powder and application guide for teams evaluating whether Grade 23 titanium powder is the right material route for lattice-enabled implant-side AM parts.

## Why spinal and porous implants point to Grade 23

These parts usually care about:

- implant-side toughness and ductility,
- tighter chemistry intent,
- lattice or porous geometry,
- a titanium route that stays inside medical-grade logic.

That is why Grade 23 keeps showing up when the discussion moves from general medical titanium to real implant programs.

Grade 23 is commonly associated with the ELI version of Ti-6Al-4V, where tighter interstitial control is part of the material logic. For spinal cages and porous implant structures, the decision usually combines chemistry, fatigue-sensitive geometry, surface condition, cleaning, and qualification evidence. Powder quality is only one part of that chain, but it is an early part that can limit the rest of the project if it is treated casually.

## Powder requirements are tied to lattice geometry

Porous implant structures are powder-sensitive because the geometry is dense with small struts, internal surfaces, and powder-removal risk. A typical discussion should cover particle-size range, oxygen level, chemistry certificate, flowability, reuse policy, and whether the powder route has already been used in the target process family.

| Implant-side concern | Powder and process implication |
| --- | --- |
| Porous or lattice architecture | Strut size, unsupported features, and trapped powder need early review. |
| Fatigue-sensitive loading | Chemistry, density, surface condition, and heat treatment must be controlled together. |
| Medical-grade logic | Grade definition and documentation matter more than generic Ti64 naming. |
| Cleaning and finishing | Internal geometry should not make powder removal or surface treatment unrealistic. |

## Why this is narrower than a broad medical page

A broad medical titanium article can include many devices. Spinal cages and porous implant structures are narrower because the part geometry, the material cleanliness, and the implant-side performance logic are all tied together.

For example, a solid surgical instrument and a porous spinal cage may both be titanium, but they are not the same powder decision. The spinal cage has more geometry-driven surface area, more cleaning sensitivity, and a stronger need to connect powder quality with build orientation, support strategy, heat treatment, and inspection.

## When Grade 23 is not the point

If the titanium part is not actually implant-side or does not need the tighter ELI-style route, a broader Ti64 path may be enough. Grade 23 should be used where the application truly needs what the tighter grade definition changes.

For industrial brackets, housings, or non-implant structural hardware, standard Ti64 powder may be the more direct starting point. For commercially pure titanium requirements, Grade 2 or TA1-style powder may be the better family. The material name should follow the application requirement rather than the other way around.

## What to define before selecting powder

A serious Grade 23 powder inquiry should define the implant class, target AM process, intended particle-size window, chemistry expectations, oxygen limit, lattice or porous dimensions, post-processing sequence, and qualification evidence needed for the project. If the part depends on a porous structure, include the minimum strut thickness, pore architecture, and cleaning assumptions. Those details are what separate a usable powder discussion from a generic titanium quote.

## The practical takeaway

Choose Ti64 Grade 23 powder for spinal cages and porous implant structures when the project needs implant-side titanium logic rather than a generic structural titanium route.
