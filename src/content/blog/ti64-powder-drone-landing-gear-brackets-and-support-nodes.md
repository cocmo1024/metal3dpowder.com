Ti64 powder becomes especially relevant when the airframe hardware is not just a light bracket, but a landing-gear support or a structural node that has to stay light while still surviving repeated loading and localized impact. These are the parts where titanium AM starts to justify itself on structural efficiency instead of novelty.

## Why drone support hardware points to Ti64

This class of part usually cares about:

- low mass,
- concentrated landing loads,
- multi-axis mounting geometry,
- fewer assembled pieces in a compact airframe zone.

That combination is one of the clearest reasons Ti64 stays relevant in UAV structural work.

## Why this is narrower than a broad aerospace bracket page

A general aerospace article can include many structural parts. Drone landing gear and support nodes are narrower because the geometry is tied to repeated landing events, local impact margin, and packaging around adjacent airframe hardware.

## When another material family is cleaner

If the structure is not heavily loaded and low cost matters more than titanium structural performance, an aluminum route may be more practical. If the geometry is simple enough for conventional machining, the AM case weakens quickly.

## The practical takeaway

Choose Ti64 powder for drone landing gear brackets and support nodes when the geometry is structurally loaded, weight-sensitive, and difficult to simplify into a basic machined part.
